## Welcome to LearnAI! 🎉

**LearnAI is your structured learning platform** for mastering AI, system design, backend engineering, and more. With comprehensive series navigation, difficulty levels, and progress tracking, you'll have everything you need to level up your skills.

According to [Stack Overflow's 2024 Developer Survey](https://stackoverflow.blog/2024/06/10/developer-survey-results-2024/), 65% of developers cite structured learning paths as crucial for skill development. That's exactly what we provide here.

## What Makes This Different?

### 📚 Structured Learning Series

Instead of random blog posts, our content is organized into **learning series** with clear prerequisites and progression:

- **AI & Machine Learning** - From neural networks to transformers
- **System Design** - Distributed systems, caching, scalability
- **Backend Engineering** - APIs, databases, authentication
- **DevOps** - Docker, Kubernetes, CI/CD pipelines
- **Frontend** - React, performance, web standards
- **Career Development** - Growth strategies, interviewing
- **Tools & Ecosystem** - IDEs, frameworks, productivity

Each series guides you from beginner to advanced concepts with hands-on examples.

### ✅ Progress Tracking

Your learning journey is automatically tracked:

- Posts are marked complete when you scroll to 80%
- Series completion percentage is calculated
- Dashboard shows your total progress
- All data saved locally in your browser

Visit [your progress dashboard](/progress/) anytime to see how far you've come.

### 🎯 Difficulty-Based Content

Every post is tagged with a difficulty level:

- **🌱 Beginner** - Fundamentals with simple examples (1,500-2,000 words)
- **📚 Intermediate** - Production-ready implementations (2,000-3,000 words)
- **🚀 Advanced** - Deep dives and optimizations (3,000-4,500 words)

Start at your level and progress at your own pace.

### 2. Follow a Series

Look for posts marked with the **"Part X of Series"** badge. These are part of structured learning paths:

1. Start with Part 1
2. Complete each part sequentially
3. Use Previous/Next navigation at top and bottom of posts
4. Track your progress with the visual progress bar

### 3. Track Your Progress

As you read posts:

- Scroll to 80% to auto-mark as complete ✅
- Or click "Mark as Complete" at the end
- View your dashboard at [`/progress/`](/progress/)
- See which series you've completed

## Example: Following a Series

Let's say you want to learn **Kubernetes**. Here's how you'd use the site:

1. **Browse** to [DevOps category](/category/devops/)
2. **Find** "Kubernetes Fundamentals" series
3. **Start** with Part 1: Architecture Overview
4. **Read** the post (about 15 minutes)
5. **Mark complete** when you finish
6. **Click "Next"** to move to Part 2: Pods and Containers
7. **Track progress** on your dashboard (e.g., "2/6 parts complete")

After completing all 6 parts, you'll have a comprehensive understanding of Kubernetes from basics to production!

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is this content free?**  
A: Yes! All content on LearnAI is completely free and always will be.

**Q: How often is new content published?**  
A: We publish 2-3 new posts per week, with at least one new series starting each month.

**Q: Can I request topics?**  
A: Absolutely! Use the [contact form](/contact/) to suggest topics you'd like to see covered.

**Q: Do I need an account to track progress?**  
A: No account needed. Progress is saved in your browser's localStorage. Just use the same browser and device.

**Q: What if I clear my browser data?**  
A: Your progress will reset. We recommend exporting your progress (feature coming soon) before clearing data.
